Generate a grounded narrative scene for an interactive story activity in a modern learning app.

Style: Believable, emotionally clear realism with clean composition, readable details, and a strong sense of place. The image should feel like a scene from a thoughtful narrative strategy game, but still grounded in real work, real people, and real environments.

Art direction:

- Keep the composition focused. Show the most important people, objects, and clues clearly.
- Favor natural lighting, calm color palettes, and modern environments over dramatic fantasy styling.
- If the prompt describes a dashboard, document, workspace, storefront, ward, factory floor, or other artifact-heavy scene, make the important evidence large enough to inspect.
- If the prompt describes a changed world state after a decision, make the consequence visible in the image itself.
- Keep the frame polished and cinematic, but not flashy or overproduced.
- Use neutral or restrained backgrounds. Avoid loud saturated colors, neon, glossy 3D, cartoon styling, and stock-photo posing.
- If text appears in the image, keep it short, legible, and useful.
- Compose around one centered primary person, artifact, screen, document, workspace detail, or visible consequence. Avoid wide side-by-side layouts unless the prompt explicitly requires comparing two states.
- If a comparison is required, stack the states vertically or place them inside one centered panel instead of spreading them across the full width.
- Keep every important character, object, clue, label, number, code line, UI control, and readable detail inside the central 80% of the frame. The outer edges may be cropped by the app.
- Leave only non-essential background, margins, or atmospheric whitespace near the outer edges.
- For dashboards, code editors, forms, tables, receipts, documents, and app screens, remove sidebars, browser chrome, file trees, toolbars, and extra columns unless they are the actual clue.
- Do not add UI chrome from the app itself. Generate only the scene.

The image should help the learner understand the situation, stakes, or consequence at a glance.
